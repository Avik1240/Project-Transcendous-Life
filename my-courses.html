Summary

The 3 Powers

We already covered the primary functions of the nervous system in the earlier section:

  1. Take in sensory information
  2. Process Information
  3. Take Action

These three purposes of the nervous system are intimately connected with what we refer to as The 3 Powers. They are:

  1. Focus
  2. Meaning
  3. Action

They are our power to ability and act. And they're the only things we can control.

Focus is how and where we dedicate our mental, emotional and physical attention, and processing power.

Meaning consists of the connotations, definitions and significance we give to what we are focusing on - experiences, people, places, things, actions, feelings, etc. It's perspective we focus on, the associations we form, and the connections we make. It's the interpretation we choose for any given thing or experience. Action is any movement towards or away from something. It's any attempt to alter our own state or the state of our environment. This includes physical actions, as well as mental and emotional actions.

Can you see how they're connected to the three purposes of the nervous system?

  1. Focus determines what sensory information we prioritize and consequently what we take in more of.
  2. Meaning shapes how we interpret the information we bring in, how we process it, and figure out what we will do with it.
  3. Actions changes our state by moving towards or away from something based on how we process the stimuli.
Meaning

Meaning refers to the connotations, definitions and significance we give to what we are focusing on - experiences, people, places, things, actions, feelings, etc. It's perspective we focus on, the associations we form, and the connections we make. It's the interpretation we choose for any give thing or experience. By choosing the meaning, we alter our subjective experiences, for better or for worse. By choosing the meaning we shape our subjective experience and shift how our brain processes the information it takes in. We can also further streamline the process of creating our vision by adding meaning to our vision and to the process of creating it. We establish, and then focus on, the "Why"s behind our vision.

Focus

Focus is the first of the 3 Powers: Focus, Meaning, & Action. Focus is how and where we dedicate our mental, emotional and physical attention, and processing power. What we choose to focus on also determines how our brain processes each experience. What we first focus on in the morning, porgrams part of our brain called the Reticular Activating System (RAS) to continually dedicate mental resources to that first focus throughout the day. We can streamline the process of creating and establishing our vision, by strengthening our focus on what we want to create.

Action

Action is internally initiated movement towards or away from something. It's any attempt to alter our own state or the state of our environment. This includes physical actions, as well as mental and emotional actions. It's how we change our object experience. Action is the ultimate goal of the nervous system and the reason why we take in and process sensory information. Action is what allows us to change, become a different person, and move towards our vision. We can use the first two powers, focus and meaning, to supercharge our actions.
